Convincing a user to sign up for a service or download an app is where UX meets marketing. I focused on words like "you" and "free" to grab the user's attention.

A user accessing the app in the store is obviously willing to make the trip, so this is really a luxury service. I acknowledge that with the phrase "treat yourself" but also let the user know it won't break the bank. It's a luxury, but it's worth it.

Words like 'fire' and 'hazard' will alert the user to danger, while 'delays' will make them think about inconvenience. I decided to tackle this challenge from both angles since sometimes people ignore warnings when they think other options will be more difficult or time consuming.
