I designed and created a prototype for this site in Adobe XD. Something I think is really interesting about UX design is that it is an iterative process and you end up tweaking things a lot as you go. You can see that the final product is a bit different from how I had originally planned things mostly because of my novice level development skills. It's a good illustration of why it's so important to work cross-functionally with the development team and various stakeholders so that everyone is on the same page about what is feasible.

I love the unique challenge of crafting informative, engaging copy with compassion and empathy in limited space. In order to improve my skills I signed up with Daily UX Writing. They sent me a daily challenge for 15 days and you can see my results below.


This is my version of the Facebook Page creation process. The biggest change is that I eliminated all of the optional steps that they currently include. Facebook makes it so easy to add those elements afterwards that it seems unneccessary to include it here. I also changed the language of the final step from 'visit your Page' to 'Finish Page'. Facebook's current version is ambiguous and a user might think they will still have an opportunity to go back or cancel.
